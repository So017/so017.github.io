My Story

Hi, I'm Sonia!

I'm a data analyst with a background in supply chain, retail operations and inventory analytics. I have always been curious about how things work, whether it is philosophy, coding, business processes, or even how to make the perfect milk foam for coffee. There is something deeply satisfying about turning curiosity into understanding.

My journey into data analytics began long before it became my job. While working in supply chain, I became curious about how shipment performance affected sales, which products performed best, and what patterns were hidden within the data. Although analysis was not a formal part of my role, I started organising data, building reports and presenting insights to my team. Seeing colleagues use those insights to make better decisions made me realise that data analytics was where my strengths and interests naturally came together.

Today, I work as an Inventory Analyst, using data to improve stock availability and operational performance across a large retail business. I analyse inventory data, investigate stock loss, build Power BI dashboards, and evaluate business initiatives that help improve inventory accuracy and support better decision-making. What I enjoy most is turning complex operational data into practical insights that make a real difference.

A few years ago, life took me in an unexpected direction when I moved to the UK and decided to run my own coffee shop. It was a completely different challenge, but one that taught me just as much. Running a business strengthened my commercial thinking, taught me to make decisions with limited information, and reminded me that behind every dataset are real customers, colleagues and experiences.

Alongside my professional work, I continue learning through personal data projects and exploring new tools and techniques. Data analytics is constantly evolving, and that is one of the things I enjoy most about it. There is always another question to explore and another opportunity to learn.

Outside work, I enjoy psychology and learning about different cultures. I am fascinated by how people think, make decisions and interact with the world around them. I have been learning Thai and Japanese, and I enjoy photography and mindfulness. Both help me slow down, notice details and appreciate the small things that often go unnoticed, a mindset that naturally carries over into data analysis.
